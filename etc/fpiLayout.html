Anchor Packaging celebrates fifty year anniversary.

Anchor Packaging is celebrating fifty years in business.  The company began operations on November 8, 1963, as a converter of plastic film and expanded operations in 1978 to include thermoforming, sheet extrusion and blown film.  Anchor has made several strategic acquisitions over the years including its recent acquisition of a Jonesboro, Arkansas manufacturing and warehousing facility from Quad Graphics.  Anchor's president, Jeff Wolf, states "our mission has been, and remains, to continually delight our customers.  This company is well-positioned for a virtually-unlimited horizon of new product possibilities, and we expect the next 50 years to continue our legacy of meeting customer requirements with innovation and packaging solutions that make them successful."  (Source:  www.qsrmagazine.com 03/18/2013)

Seattle's Best plans drive-thru-only units in Dallas.

Seattle's Best Coffee plans to open fifteen new drive-thru-only locations in the Dallas-Fort Worth area this year.  The chain has been testing the small-footprint drive-thrus in Seattle and Chicago over the past year.  Last year, parent company Starbucks Corp., said it planned to expand Seattle's Best as a drive-thru brand to accommodate the needs of customers who want coffee on the go.  "We believe that everyone deserves a great cup of coffee, even if they don't have time to sit in a cafe," said Frank Sica, vice president and general manager for Seattle's Best.  The tested drive-thru-only units tested in Seattle offer "dashboard friendly" food, along with coffee and other drinks.  The menu will include egg sandwiches, pretzel melts and sweet and savory "anytime pies."  The drive-thrus will also offer a value menu with combo meals under five dollars.  The company says Dallas is ideal for the expansion because, according to surveys, area residents eat out 70 percent more than the national average.  (Source:  www.nrn.com , 03/18/2013)
